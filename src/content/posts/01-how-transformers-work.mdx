Every AI model you use today — ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini — is built on the same foundation.
It is called the transformer. Understanding it changes how you see everything else.

## What is a token

Before a model reads your words, it breaks them into pieces called tokens.
A token is roughly four characters. "Hello" is one token. "transformer" is two.

This matters because models do not think in words. They think in numbers.
Each token becomes a number, and the model works with those numbers.

## What is attention

Here is the core idea behind transformers.

When you read the sentence "The bank was steep and muddy," you know "bank" means a riverbank, not a financial institution.
You know that because of the words around it. "Steep" and "muddy" give you context.

Transformers do the same thing. For every token, the model asks one question: which other tokens matter most right now?

That process is called attention.

## Why this was a breakthrough

Before transformers, models read text left to right, one word at a time.
They forgot earlier context by the time they reached the end of a long sentence.

Transformers read the entire sequence at once.
Every token can attend to every other token simultaneously.
That parallel processing is why transformers are both more accurate and faster to train.

## What this means in practice

The attention mechanism is why Claude can follow a complex 10,000-word document.
It is why a model can answer a question about something mentioned 50 paragraphs earlier.
The model is not scanning through text. It is weighing relationships across the whole thing at once.
